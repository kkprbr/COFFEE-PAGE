About

The third wave coffee movement, which originated in the United States in the early 2000s, emphasizes high-quality, artisanal coffee made with precision and care. Third wave cafés focus on sourcing specialty beans from specific regions, roasting them in-house, and brewing them using methods such as pour-over, AeroPress, or siphon. These cafés often have minimalist, industrial-chic interiors and a menu that highlights single-origin coffees, espresso-based drinks, and alternative milk options. Beyond coffee, third wave cafés may also offer gourmet pastries, small-batch teas, and locally sourced snacks, catering to discerning coffee connoisseurs and food enthusiasts alike.
